DIABETES MELLITUS CLINICAL GUIDELINES 2024

DEFINITION:
Diabetes mellitus is a group of metabolic diseases characterized by hyperglycemia resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both.

CLASSIFICATION:
Type 1 Diabetes: Absolute insulin deficiency due to autoimmune destruction of beta cells
Type 2 Diabetes: Progressive loss of insulin secretion with insulin resistance
Gestational Diabetes: Diabetes diagnosed during pregnancy
Prediabetes: Fasting glucose 100-125 mg/dL or HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA:
Fasting Plasma Glucose >= 126 mg/dL
2-hour Plasma Glucose >= 200 mg/dL during OGTT
HbA1c >= 6.5%
Random Plasma Glucose >= 200 mg/dL with symptoms

TREATMENT TARGETS:
HbA1c < 7.0% for most adults
HbA1c < 8.0% for elderly or high risk patients
Fasting glucose 80-130 mg/dL
Postprandial glucose < 180 mg/dL
Blood pressure < 130/80 mmHg
LDL cholesterol < 100 mg/dL

FIRST LINE MEDICATIONS:
1. Metformin 500-2000mg daily (first line for Type 2)
2. GLP-1 agonists (Semaglutide, Liraglutide) for weight loss and cardiovascular benefit
3. SGLT2 inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin) for heart failure and kidney protection
4. DPP-4 inhibitors (Sitagliptin, Saxagliptin) for low hypoglycemia risk
5. Sulfonylureas (Glipizide, Glimepiride) for cost-effective glucose lowering
6. Insulin therapy when oral agents are insufficient

INSULIN TYPES:
Rapid-acting: Lispro, Aspart, Glulisine (onset 15 min)
Short-acting: Regular insulin (onset 30-60 min)
Intermediate: NPH insulin (onset 2-4 hours)
Long-acting: Glargine, Detemir, Degludec (onset 1-2 hours, 24hr duration)

MONITORING:
HbA1c every 3 months until stable then every 6 months
Fasting glucose daily for insulin users
Annual lipid panel
Annual kidney function (creatinine, eGFR, urine albumin)
Annual eye exam for diabetic retinopathy
Annual foot examination for neuropathy and vascular disease
Annual blood pressure measurement

COMPLICATIONS AND SCREENING:
Diabetic Nephropathy: Screen with urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio annually
Diabetic Retinopathy: Annual dilated eye exam
Diabetic Neuropathy: Annual 10g monofilament foot exam
Cardiovascular Disease: High risk, use statin therapy
Diabetic Foot: Inspect feet at every visit

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:
Medical nutrition therapy with carbohydrate counting
150 minutes moderate aerobic exercise per week
Resistance training 2-3 times per week
Weight loss of 5-10% body weight if overweight
Smoking cessation
Limit alcohol to 1 drink per day for women, 2 for men

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT:
Mild: 15g fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tablets, juice)
Severe: Glucagon 1mg IM or SC, or IV dextrose
Target glucose > 70 mg/dL
Rule of 15: 15g carbs, check glucose after 15 minutes

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:
Elderly: Avoid tight control, target HbA1c 7.5-8.5%
Pregnancy: Target HbA1c < 6.0%, use insulin only
Kidney Disease: Avoid Metformin if eGFR < 30
Heart Failure: Prefer SGLT2 inhibitors
