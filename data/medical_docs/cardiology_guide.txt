CARDIOLOGY CLINICAL GUIDELINES 2024

ACUTE MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION (AMI)

DEFINITION:
AMI is defined as myocardial cell death due to prolonged ischemia, characterized by elevated cardiac biomarkers with clinical evidence of ischemia.

STEMI DIAGNOSIS:
ST elevation > 1mm in 2 contiguous limb leads
ST elevation > 2mm in 2 contiguous precordial leads
New left bundle branch block (LBBB)
Posterior MI: ST depression V1-V3 with dominant R wave

NSTEMI DIAGNOSIS:
Elevated troponin without ST elevation
ST depression or T wave changes
Symptoms of ischemia

BIOMARKERS:
Troponin I or T: Rises 3-6 hours, peaks 12-24 hours, normalizes 7-14 days
CK-MB: Rises 4-6 hours, peaks 12-24 hours, normalizes 48-72 hours
BNP/NT-proBNP: Elevated in heart failure

STEMI MANAGEMENT:
Primary PCI within 90 minutes of first medical contact (preferred)
Fibrinolysis within 30 minutes if PCI not available within 120 minutes
Aspirin 325mg immediately
P2Y12 inhibitor (Ticagrelor 180mg or Clopidogrel 600mg)
Anticoagulation with Heparin or Bivalirudin
Beta-blocker within 24 hours if no contraindications
ACE inhibitor or ARB within 24 hours for LVEF < 40%
Statin therapy high-intensity (Atorvastatin 80mg)

NSTEMI MANAGEMENT:
Antiplatelet therapy: Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor
Anticoagulation: Heparin, Fondaparinux, or Bivalirudin
Early invasive strategy within 24 hours for high risk
Beta-blocker, ACE inhibitor, statin therapy
Nitroglycerin for ongoing chest pain

HEART FAILURE

CLASSIFICATION:
HFrEF: EF < 40% (reduced ejection fraction)
HFmrEF: EF 40-49% (mildly reduced)
HFpEF: EF >= 50% (preserved)

NYHA CLASSIFICATION:
Class I: No symptoms with ordinary activity
Class II: Slight limitation with ordinary activity
Class III: Marked limitation, comfortable only at rest
Class IV: Symptoms at rest

HEART FAILURE WITH REDUCED EF TREATMENT:
ACE inhibitor or ARB (Enalapril, Lisinopril, Valsartan)
Beta-blocker (Carvedilol, Metoprolol succinate, Bisoprolol)
Mineralocorticoid antagonist (Spironolactone, Eplerenone)
SGLT2 inhibitor (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan) for persistent symptoms
Loop diuretic (Furosemide) for fluid overload
ICD for EF < 35% despite 3 months optimal therapy
CRT for EF < 35% with LBBB and QRS > 150ms

ATRIAL FIBRILLATION

TYPES:
Paroxysmal: Episodes terminate within 7 days
Persistent: Episodes lasting > 7 days
Long-standing persistent: > 12 months
Permanent: Accepted as permanent rhythm

STROKE RISK ASSESSMENT (CHA2DS2-VASc):
Congestive heart failure (1 point)
Hypertension (1 point)
Age >= 75 years (2 points)
Diabetes (1 point)
Stroke/TIA history (2 points)
Vascular disease (1 point)
Age 65-74 years (1 point)
Sex category female (1 point)
Score >= 2 men or >= 3 women: Anticoagulation recommended

ANTICOAGULATION:
DOACs preferred over warfarin
Apixaban 5mg twice daily
Rivaroxaban 20mg daily
Dabigatran 150mg twice daily
Warfarin INR 2.0-3.0 if DOAC contraindicated

RATE CONTROL:
Target heart rate < 110 bpm at rest
Beta-blockers (Metoprolol, Atenolol)
Calcium channel blockers (Diltiazem, Verapamil)
Digoxin for heart failure patients

HYPERTROPHIC CARDIOMYOPATHY:
Avoid dehydration and strenuous exercise
Beta-blockers first line for symptoms
Septal reduction therapy for refractory symptoms
ICD for high risk sudden cardiac death
