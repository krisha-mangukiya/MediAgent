HYPERTENSION CLINICAL GUIDELINES 2024

DEFINITION:
Hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure >= 130 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure >= 80 mmHg based on the 2024 ACC/AHA guidelines.

CLASSIFICATION:
Normal: Systolic < 120 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg
Elevated: Systolic 120-129 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg
Stage 1 Hypertension: Systolic 130-139 mmHg or Diastolic 80-89 mmHg
Stage 2 Hypertension: Systolic >= 140 mmHg or Diastolic >= 90 mmHg
Hypertensive Crisis: Systolic > 180 mmHg and/or Diastolic > 120 mmHg

FIRST LINE TREATMENT:
1. Thiazide diuretics (e.g., Hydrochlorothiazide 25mg daily)
2. ACE inhibitors (e.g., Lisinopril 10mg daily)
3. Calcium channel blockers (e.g., Amlodipine 5mg daily)
4. ARBs (e.g., Losartan 50mg daily)

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:
Reduce sodium intake to less than 2.3g per day
DASH diet recommended
Regular aerobic exercise 150 minutes per week
Limit alcohol consumption
Smoking cessation
Weight reduction if BMI > 25

MONITORING:
Blood pressure should be checked every 3 months after starting treatment
Annual kidney function tests (creatinine, eGFR)
Annual ECG for left ventricular hypertrophy assessment
Ophthalmology review annually for hypertensive retinopathy

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:
Diabetes: Target BP < 130/80 mmHg, ACE inhibitors preferred
Chronic Kidney Disease: Target BP < 130/80 mmHg, ACE inhibitors or ARBs preferred
Elderly (>65 years): Start with lower doses, target BP < 140/90 mmHg
Pregnancy: Avoid ACE inhibitors and ARBs, use Methyldopa or Labetalol

EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT:
Hypertensive urgency (>180/120 without organ damage):
Oral medications, reduce BP over 24-48 hours
Amlodipine or Labetalol oral

Hypertensive emergency (>180/120 with organ damage):
IV medications required
Nicardipine IV or Labetalol IV
Reduce BP by maximum 25% in first hour
