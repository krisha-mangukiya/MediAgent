DRUG INTERACTIONS AND PHARMACOLOGY REFERENCE 2024

COMMON DRUG INTERACTIONS

CARDIOVASCULAR DRUGS:

ACE Inhibitors (Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril):
Interaction with NSAIDs: Reduced antihypertensive effect, increased renal impairment risk
Interaction with Potassium supplements: Risk of hyperkalemia
Interaction with Lithium: Increased lithium toxicity
Interaction with Aliskiren: Contraindicated in diabetes or renal impairment
Contraindicated in: Pregnancy, bilateral renal artery stenosis, history of angioedema

Beta-Blockers (Metoprolol, Atenolol, Carvedilol):
Interaction with Verapamil/Diltiazem: Risk of complete heart block
Interaction with Insulin/Sulfonylureas: Masks hypoglycemia symptoms
Interaction with Clonidine: Rebound hypertension on withdrawal
Contraindicated in: Severe asthma, decompensated heart failure, sick sinus syndrome

Statins (Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin, Simvastatin):
Interaction with Amiodarone: Increased statin levels, myopathy risk
Interaction with Cyclosporine: Increased statin toxicity
Interaction with Clarithromycin: Increased rhabdomyolysis risk
Simvastatin + Amlodipine: Maximum Simvastatin dose 20mg

Warfarin:
Interaction with Aspirin: Increased bleeding risk
Interaction with NSAIDs: Increased bleeding risk
Interaction with Amiodarone: Significantly increases INR
Interaction with Antibiotics: Alters gut flora, changes INR
Interaction with Vitamin K foods: Reduces anticoagulation effect
Target INR: 2.0-3.0 for AF, 2.5-3.5 for mechanical valves

DIABETES DRUGS:

Metformin:
Interaction with Contrast dye: Hold 48 hours before and after IV contrast
Interaction with Alcohol: Increased lactic acidosis risk
Interaction with ACE inhibitors: Rare risk of lactic acidosis
Contraindicated: eGFR < 30 mL/min, liver failure, heart failure requiring hospitalization

SGLT2 Inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin):
Interaction with Diuretics: Enhanced diuresis, dehydration risk
Interaction with Insulin: Increased DKA risk if insulin dose reduced rapidly
Side effects: UTI, genital mycotic infections, DKA risk
Contraindicated: eGFR < 30, Type 1 diabetes

GLP-1 Agonists (Semaglutide, Liraglutide):
Interaction with Oral medications: Slows gastric emptying, reduces absorption
Side effects: Nausea, vomiting, pancreatitis risk
Contraindicated: Personal or family history of MTC or MEN2

Sulfonylureas (Glipizide, Glimepiride):
Interaction with Fluconazole: Increased hypoglycemia risk
Interaction with Beta-blockers: Masks hypoglycemia symptoms
Interaction with NSAIDs: Enhanced hypoglycemic effect
Risk of weight gain and hypoglycemia

ANTIBIOTICS:

Fluoroquinolones (Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin):
Interaction with Antacids/Iron: Reduces antibiotic absorption
Interaction with Warfarin: Significantly increases INR
Interaction with Antidiabetics: Risk of hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia
Risk: QT prolongation, tendon rupture, C. difficile

Macrolides (Clarithromycin, Azithromycin):
Interaction with Statins: Increased myopathy risk
Interaction with Warfarin: Increases INR
Interaction with QT prolonging drugs: Additive QT prolongation risk

PAIN MEDICATIONS:

NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen, Diclofenac):
Interaction with ACE inhibitors/ARBs: Reduced antihypertensive effect, renal impairment
Interaction with Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk
Interaction with Aspirin: Reduced cardioprotective effect of aspirin
Interaction with Lithium: Increased lithium toxicity
Interaction with Corticosteroids: Increased GI bleeding risk
Contraindicated: Active peptic ulcer, severe renal impairment, heart failure

Opioids (Morphine, Oxycodone, Tramadol):
Interaction with Benzodiazepines: Respiratory depression, death risk
Interaction with MAOIs: Serotonin syndrome
Interaction with CNS depressants: Additive sedation
Tramadol + SSRIs: Serotonin syndrome risk

HIGH RISK MEDICATIONS:

QT Prolonging Drugs (avoid combination):
Amiodarone, Sotalol, Haloperidol, Methadone, Azithromycin, Ciprofloxacin, Ondansetron
Risk: Torsades de pointes, ventricular fibrillation

Nephrotoxic Drugs (monitor renal function):
NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors in renovascular disease, Aminoglycosides, Vancomycin, Contrast dye

Hepatotoxic Drugs (monitor liver function):
Statins, Methotrexate, Isoniazid, Valproate, Azathioprine
